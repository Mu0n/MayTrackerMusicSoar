About the Event

May Tracker Music Soar celebrates the golden era of vintage computing and the magic of tracker music. Whether you're a seasoned tracker composer or a curious newcomer, this event is for you! Our core values include positivity, inclusivity, and a shared passion for creating music.

What Is Tracker Music?

Tracker music emerged in the late 1980s and early 1990s, fueled by home computers like the Commodore Amiga. It's a unique way of composing music using digital samples arranged in patterns. Our focus? The iconic MOD, S3M, and IT formats that once ruled the scene.

FAQ

  • What skill set is necessary?

    • Anyone can participate. You can make the event a learning experience on how to do tracker music. You can use it to polish your pre-existing skills. You can use it to remove the rust off what you did 28 years ago. This event is for fun.

  • Do I need special equipment?

    • While some trackers can let you use midi controllers to input the notes, it isn't necessary. Just a modern computer with a keyboard will do. It can be 100% through a modern software (including but not limited to Fast Tracker II). If it strikes your fancy, you can go back to vintage machines and use software for those.

  • Looks cool but I don't think I can commit to do a full song.

    • Just do one riff. Or one bass line. Or one beat pattern. It doesn't matter. As long as you have fun.

  • Is style x or y is acceptable?

    • Do whatever style of music you want. Use whatever level of cheese you want. As long as you have fun.

  • I'm afraid my project won't be finished / I only get free time after the month of May.

    • Galaxy brain moment: just do it all year long. As long as you have fun.

  • Where do I get instruments and sample sound files?

    • Record your own or steal from other mod files. No one will care. As long as you have fun.
